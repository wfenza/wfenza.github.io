Transparent Pricing

Contingency Fee Cases

Most personal injury matters can be handled on a contingency fee basis, which means that if you have been injured, unless and until you are compensated, you pay no attorney fees (though you do pay the costs of litigation as stated above). Payment is instead made as a percentage of your recovery. Accepting cases on a contingency fee basis is discretionary and is not guaranteed to any potential client.


For Pennsylvania cases, the contingency fee is ⅓ of your recovery.


In New Jersey, contingency fees are governed by the Rules of Professional Conduct. Contingency fees in New Jersey cases are:

  • 33⅓% on the first $750,000 recovered;
  • 30% on the next $750,000 recovered;
  • 25% on the next $750,000 recovered;
  • 20% on the next $750,000; and
  • a reasonable amount to be set by the court on all recoveries in excess of $3 million.
